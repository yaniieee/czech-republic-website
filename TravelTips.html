Czech Republic Travel Tips

Travel Tips for Czechia

  • Best Time to Visit: The best time to visit Czechia is during the spring (April to June) and fall (September to October) when the weather is mild and the tourist crowds are smaller.
  • Visa Requirements: Check if you need a visa to enter Czechia. Citizens of the EU, USA, Canada, and Australia can enter without a visa for short stays.
  • Health and Safety: Ensure you have travel insurance. Healthcare in Czechia is of high quality, but it's advisable to have insurance for emergencies.
  • Local Cuisine: Don't miss trying traditional Czech dishes like goulash, svíčková (marinated beef with creamy sauce), and trdelník (a sweet pastry).
  • Public Transport: Czechia has an efficient public transport system, including trains and buses. Consider getting a travel pass for unlimited rides.
  • Language: While many people in tourist areas speak English, learning a few basic phrases in Czech can be helpful. You can say "Děkuji" = Thank you and "Pivo" = Beer
  • Currency: The currency is the Czech koruna (CZK). Credit cards are widely accepted, but it's good to have some cash for smaller establishments.
  • Tipping:Tipping is customary in restaurants and cafes. A tip of around 10% is appreciated.
  • Transportation: Public transportation in cities like Prague is efficient and affordable. Consider getting a travel pass for unlimited rides.
  • Safety: Czechia is generally safe for travelers, but be cautious of pickpockets in crowded areas.
  • Cultural Etiquette: Respect local customs and traditions. When visiting churches or religious sites, dress modestly.
  • Emergency Numbers: In case of emergencies, dial 112 for general emergencies or 158 for police assistance.
